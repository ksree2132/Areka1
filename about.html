About

Areca nut cultivation is economically important in regions where it is grown. The nuts are harvested and sold for various purposes, including traditional use, medicinal applications, and in the preparation of products like paan (a chewable preparation with areca nut, betel leaves, and other ingredients).In traditional medicine, areca nut has been used for various purposes, including its perceived digestive and stimulant properties. However, its use in traditional medicine is not without controversy, given the associated health risks. Betel chewing has cultural significance in many communities, often playing a role in ceremonies, social events, and traditional practices. The act of offering betel quid can be a gesture of hospitality and friendship in some cultures.
